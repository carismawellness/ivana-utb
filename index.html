Honest check-in

You don't need another person to tell you something's off. You already feel it.

  • You wake up tired even after a full night's sleep — not physically, but emotionally. Like you've been carrying something heavy in your dreams.
  • You've been strong for so long that you've forgotten what it feels like to just… be. Without holding it together for everyone else.
  • You find yourself reaching for food, your phone, or a glass of wine — not because you want it, but because sitting with how you actually feel is unbearable.
  • There's a low hum of anxiety in your chest that never fully goes away. You've learned to function around it, but it's exhausting.
  • You look at your life from the outside and think "I should be grateful" — but inside, something feels hollow. Missing. Like you're watching your own life from behind glass.
  • You've been through something — a breakup, a loss, a shift — and you haven't fully landed yet. You're in-between, and the in-between is the loneliest place.
  • You catch yourself saying "I'm fine" more times a day than you can count. And every time, you feel the distance between that word and the truth.
  • You've tried therapy, journaling, meditation apps, self-help books — and they helped, a little. But nothing has touched the deeper thing you can't quite name.

If you read even one of those and felt a quiet "yes" — this workshop was made for you.

A different possibility

What if the thing you've been searching for isn't outside you — but underneath everything you've piled on top of it?

  • What if you could feel calm in your own body — not the forced kind, but the kind that comes from finally understanding why you've been so restless?
  • What if the anxiety, the emotional eating, the overthinking — what if none of it meant you were broken? What if it was your body trying to speak to you in the only language it had left?
  • What if you could stop performing "fine" and actually start feeling it — not perfectly, not all at once, but genuinely?
  • What if there was a way to hold both the pain and the peace — without choosing, without pretending, without numbing?
  • What if the wholeness you've been looking for has been there the entire time, quietly waiting for you to stop long enough to notice?

This workshop is the first step toward finding out.

What you'll explore

Three ideas that might change how you see yourself.

Pillar One

You're Not Broken — You're Disconnected

The myth of brokenness is the first thing we need to unlearn.

You've probably been told — by culture, by relationships, by the voice in your own head — that something about you needs fixing. That if you could just lose the weight, find the right partner, get the promotion, manage the anxiety… then you'd finally feel okay.

But what if you were never broken in the first place? What if you've just been disconnected from the part of you that already knows you're whole?

You'll walk away with: A new understanding of why "fixing yourself" has never worked — and what to do instead.

Pillar Two

Your Body Is Speaking — Are You Listening?

The anxiety, the exhaustion, the cravings — they're not the problem. They're the message.

Your body has been trying to tell you something for a long time. The tightness in your chest. The way you eat when you're not hungry. The headaches that come every Sunday night. The tears that arrive out of nowhere.

These aren't signs that something is wrong with you. They're signs that something inside you is asking to be heard.

You'll walk away with: A simple way to recognize what your body is actually telling you — and how to respond with compassion instead of control.

Pillar Three

From Control to Trust — The Path to Inner Peace

The harder you grip, the more life resists. There's another way.

Most of us were taught that safety comes from control. Control the food. Control the emotions. Control the outcomes. But the more you try to hold everything in place, the more exhausted you become — and the further you drift from peace.

Inner peace isn't something you achieve. It's something you allow. And it starts with learning to trust — yourself, your body, and the timing of your own life.

You'll walk away with: A shift from "I need to hold it all together" to "I can let go and still be safe" — and a practice to anchor that shift.

What you'll take home

This isn't a lecture. You'll leave with real tools you can use tonight.

  • A 60-second breathing technique — for when anxiety rises, whether you're in your car, in bed, or in the middle of a workday.
  • A new understanding of your patterns — why you reach for food, wine, your phone, or sleep when emotions get too big, and what your body is actually asking for.
  • A 5-minute daily practice — to reconnect with yourself. No app. No equipment. No performance. Just you and a few minutes of honesty.
  • A different lens on your mental health — one that sees you as a whole person having a deeply human experience, not a diagnosis or a list of symptoms.
  • A room full of women who get it — and the quiet relief of realizing you are not alone in this.

Ivana spent over seven years working closely with women as a wellness consultant — helping them navigate emotional eating, body image, and the complicated relationship between how we feel and how we cope. Her own journey took her through cultural displacement, burnout, and the kind of quiet unraveling that doesn't look dramatic from the outside but reshapes everything on the inside. She didn't find her way through by mastering a technique. She found it by learning to listen — to her body, to her patterns, and to the parts of herself she'd been avoiding.

Now she's writing a book about it — Underneath the Brokenness: The Life Manual I Wish Came with My Birth Certificate — and bringing that same wisdom into rooms where women can experience it together, not just read about it.

Ivana bridges psychology and spirituality — giving you both the understanding of WHY you feel this way and the tools to shift it.

She's not a guru. She's not a celebrity. She's a woman who has been where you are — and found her way through.

Questions you might have

Before you decide.

Is this a religious event?

No. When Ivana talks about spirituality, she means your connection to yourself — your inner world, your intuition, the part of you that knows things before your mind catches up. This is not tied to any religion, belief system, or practice. You don't need to believe in anything specific. You just need to be open.

Do I need experience with meditation or spiritual practice?

Not at all. This workshop is designed for women who are just beginning to explore these ideas — or who have never explored them and aren't sure where to start. There's no chanting. No incense. No pretending. Just honest conversation, simple tools, and real insight.

Is this going to be a sales pitch?

No. This is a full, complete workshop. You'll leave with real tools and a different perspective. At the very end, Ivana will briefly share how you can continue this work with her if you choose to — but there's absolutely no pressure. Most people come, get what they need, and that's enough.

What if I'm not sure this is for me?

If you've read this far and something in these words made you pause — if even one sentence felt like it was describing your life — trust that feeling. That quiet recognition is exactly what we'll be exploring together.

Is it really free?

Yes. This is Ivana's way of introducing herself and her work to women in Malta. She believes the first step should never have a price tag. Come, experience it, and decide for yourself if this resonates.
