03 — Customer Archetypes

The Five Women You’ll Serve

Five distinct archetypes represent your core audience. Most women are a blend of 2–3 archetypes. Understanding their inner world, pain points, and language is key to everything: ad copy, workshop content, and program design.

1. The Exhausted Achiever
She has everything on paper — and nothing inside.

Who She Is

Age 32–42. Career woman, possibly with children. Works in finance, legal, tech, or iGaming in Malta. High-functioning anxiety. Reaches for wine at 8pm.

Her Pain

Exhausted by performing “fine.” Feels guilty for not being grateful. The gap between her outer life and inner life is growing. Sunday night dread.

What She Needs to Hear

“You’re not broken. You’ve just forgotten who you are underneath it all.” — This is the headline she’ll screenshot.

Conversion Path

Workshop: 85% Group: 65% VIP: 30%

“I have a beautiful life. So why do I feel like I’m watching it from behind glass?”
2. The Post-Breakup / Loss Woman
She’s in the in-between — and the in-between is the loneliest place.

Who She Is

Age 28–45. Going through or recently emerged from divorce, breakup, loss of a parent, or miscarriage. Still finding her footing. Possibly new to Malta (expat).

Her Pain

Identity crisis — who is she without the relationship? Loneliness in a small island where everyone seems coupled. The grief that nobody sees.

What She Needs to Hear

“You don’t have to have it all figured out to show up.” — Permission to be unfinished.

Conversion Path

Workshop: 90% Group: 70% VIP: 35%

“Everyone keeps saying I’ll be fine. But nobody asks how I actually am.”
3. The Emotional Eater
She knows it was never about the food. She just doesn’t know what it IS about.

Who She Is

Age 30–50. Has tried every diet — keto, intermittent fasting, Weight Watchers, Carisma Slimming. Yo-yo pattern. Body image issues. Ivana’s core expertise area.

Her Pain

Shame cycle: eat → guilt → restrict → binge → shame. The food is the symptom, not the problem. She suspects this but can’t name it.

What She Needs to Hear

“The chocolate at 9pm isn’t hunger. It’s a feeling you don’t have words for yet.” — Validation without judgment.

Conversion Path

Workshop: 80% Group: 60% VIP: 25%

“I don’t need another meal plan. I need someone to understand why I keep doing this.”
4. The Anxious People-Pleaser
She says yes to everyone — except herself.

Who She Is

Age 25–40. Often in caregiving roles (teacher, nurse, HR, admin). Maltese cultural expectation to be the “good daughter.” Low hum of anxiety. Terrified of being “selfish.”

Her Pain

Burnout approaching. Cannot say no. Feels invisible. The anxiety sits in her chest all day. She’s functioning but slowly drowning.

What She Needs to Hear

“You’ve been strong for so long that you’ve forgotten what it feels like to just... be.” — Recognition of her pattern.

Conversion Path

Workshop: 75% Group: 55% VIP: 20%

“I catch myself saying ‘I’m fine’ ten times a day. And every time, I feel the distance between that word and the truth.”
5. The Spiritual Seeker
She feels the pull toward something deeper — she just doesn’t know where to go.

Who She Is

Age 35–55. Has dabbled in yoga, meditation, crystals, tarot. “Spiritual but not religious.” May be a lapsed Catholic. Looking for structure, not dogma.

Her Pain

Fragmented practice — bits of everything, nothing coherent. Craves a guide, not a guru. Malta’s Catholic culture makes her feel she can’t talk about this openly.

What She Needs to Hear

“This is not tied to any religion. It’s your connection to yourself — your intuition, the part of you that knows things before your mind catches up.”

Conversion Path

Workshop: 70% Group: 50% VIP: 40%

“I’ve been collecting tools for years — crystals, journals, apps. But nothing has given me what I actually need: a place to belong.”
Cross-Archetype Insight: Most women are a blend of 2–3 archetypes. The Exhausted Achiever who also emotionally eats. The People-Pleaser who is also going through a breakup. The landing page copy is written to speak across archetypes simultaneously — each bullet in “Is This You?” targets a different archetype while feeling personal to all.
04 — Go-to-Market Strategy

Three-Phase Market Entry

The go-to-market follows a “land, expand, scale” framework adapted for a solo practitioner in a micro-market. Do not advance phases until success criteria are met.

1
Months 1–3

Validate — “Prove the Model”

Run 1–2 workshops. Fill first coaching cohort (even 4–6 women). Collect 3–5 testimonials. Document every metric.

Budget: €500–800 total • Go/No-Go: 15+ attendees, 3+ paying clients, 80%+ completion

Channel Mix: 40% personal network • 30% Meta ads (€150–200) • 20% Instagram organic • 10% WhatsApp

2
Months 4–6

Establish — “Build the Engine”

1 workshop/month on a predictable cadence. Increase ad spend to €300–400. Build partnerships. Launch email nurture.

Budget: €650/month • Targets: 20+ attendees, 12–16% conversion, €1,300+ monthly profit

Channel Mix: 35% Meta ads • 25% Instagram • 15% Email • 15% Partnerships • 10% Word of mouth

3
Months 7–12

Scale — “Maximize the Asset”

2 workshops/month. Raise prices (Group €597, VIP €1,197). Launch 1:1 coaching. Publish the book. Explore online format.

Budget: €1,100/month • Targets: €4,000–6,000/month revenue, 300+ email list, 10+ testimonials

Channel Mix: 30% Meta ads • 25% Content • 15% Book PR • 15% Partnerships • 15% Referrals

07 — 90-Day Launch Plan

From Zero to First Cohort

Three 30-day blocks, each with a clear objective. Follow in sequence. Do not skip steps.

Month 1: Foundation

Objective: Set up all infrastructure. Launch organic presence. Prepare for first workshop.

  • Register as self-employed in Malta (Jobsplus + Commissioner for Revenue)
  • Open separate business bank account + Stripe account
  • Set up MailerLite, Instagram Business, WhatsApp Business
  • Purchase domain (ivana.mt or underneaththebrokenness.com)
  • Build landing page for workshop registration
  • Create 9 Instagram posts (3 weeks of content) in Canva
  • Write workshop outline and rehearse full 90-minute run-through
  • Identify and book venue (3–4 weeks out)
  • Create first Meta ad creative (1 image + 1 selfie video)
  • Write 3 ad copy versions and launch campaign (€10–15/day)
  • Send “save the date” to personal network
  • Set up 4 email templates (confirmation, 7-day, 3-day, 1-day reminders)

Month 2: Conversion & Delivery

Objective: Convert workshop attendees. Begin delivering 8-week program. Collect first testimonials.

  • Execute 7-day post-workshop email + WhatsApp conversion sequence
  • Personal WhatsApp follow-up with warm leads (voice notes, not text)
  • Process payments and onboard coaching clients
  • Send welcome emails, workbook, audio practices, WhatsApp group invite
  • Deliver weekly 90-min group sessions + individual voice note check-ins
  • VIP 1:1 sessions on separate day
  • Begin planning Workshop #2 (4–6 weeks out)
  • Record all metrics: registrations, attendees, conversions, revenue, CPL

Month 3: Second Cycle & Refinement

Objective: Launch second workshop. Refine. Complete first cohort. Collect testimonials.

  • Create new ad creative (test a different angle)
  • Launch Meta ads for Workshop 2 (increase budget if Workshop 1 performed well)
  • Use first testimonials in ads and landing page
  • Activate referral requests with current coaching clients
  • Continue program delivery (deepest emotional phase — increase check-in frequency)
  • Deliver Workshop 2 with all learnings applied
  • Collect informal testimonials (WhatsApp quotes with permission)
  • Quarterly business review: revenue, CAC, conversion, retention, energy level

08 — Risk Assessment

Key Risks & Mitigation

Nothing in this plan is risk-free. The following risks are acknowledged and mitigated.

High

Workshops don’t fill (<15 attendees)

Lower threshold to 10. Boost organic. Activate personal network. Push date back 1 week. 8 women is intimate, not empty.

High

Conversion rate below 10%

Diagnose root cause. Change ONE variable per cycle. Add payment plans. Add qualifying question to registration.

High

Ivana burnout / energy depletion

Cap at 1 workshop/month in Phase 1–2. Protect Sundays. Build buffer between sessions. Hire VA when revenue supports it.

Med

Client mental health crisis

Crisis protocol: validate, acknowledge boundary, refer to Malta services (Richmond Foundation 1770, Supportline 179). Maintain coaching-vs-therapy distinction.

Med

Negative feedback / public review

HEAR framework: Hear, Evaluate, Act, Respond with grace. Never engage in public arguments. Never delete comments unless personal attacks.

Med

Pricing objection blocks conversion

Payment plans (€275 × 2). Per-session reframing (€62/week). Two-session money-back guarantee. Workshop early bird discount.

Med

Meta ads underperform

Diversify: grow organic Instagram, activate partnerships, increase referral investment. Test new ad angles quarterly.

Low

Competitor enters with similar positioning

Deepen moat: publish book faster, build alumni community, secure partnership exclusivities, double down on content quality.

Revenue Shortfall Contingency: If Year 1 revenue tracks below €15K by Month 6: cut ad spend to organic-only, launch 1:1 coaching at €150/session, add “refresher” cohort for past participants at €197, test smaller bi-weekly workshops, seek corporate wellness bookings.
09 — Competitive Moat

Five Layers of Defensibility

In a low-barrier market, Ivana’s defensibility is built on accumulated advantages that are difficult to replicate quickly.

1

Dual-Lens Positioning

Psychology + spirituality integrated. Requires genuine fluency in both domains. 7 years of clinical-adjacent work + experiential spiritual practice. Cannot be faked or quickly acquired.

2

The Book as Authority Asset

Underneath the Brokenness = credibility accelerator, content engine, lead magnet, curriculum foundation, and PR asset. Years of lived experience cannot be replicated with a weekend cert.

3

Intimate Format & Community

8–12 women per cohort creates depth, safety, alumni bonds, and natural scarcity. By Cohort 5–6: 40–60+ women in a connected Malta tribe. No competitor can manufacture this.

4

Cultural Bridge (Serbian-Maltese)

Outsider-insider position: names cultural patterns locals can’t see. Her immigration/identity story mirrors her clients’ emotional displacement. Personal history cannot be copied.

5

Trust Compounding in a Micro-Market

In 530K people, reputation IS the competitive advantage. Within 12–18 months: “the woman in Malta who does that workshop” = category ownership. First-mover in a niche creates natural monopoly.
